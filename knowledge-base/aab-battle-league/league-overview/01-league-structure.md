# League Structure and Organization

**Category**: League Overview  
**Purpose**: Comprehensive overview of the AAB Battle League organization and structure

---

## Introduction

The AAB Battle League is a competitive Pokemon battle league organized around structured seasons, team-based competition, and a sophisticated draft system. The league operates through a combination of Google Sheets for data management and a programmatic application for battle tracking and standings.

---

## League Organization

### Conferences and Divisions

The league is organized into two conferences, each containing multiple divisions:

**Lance Conference**
- Kanto Division
- Johto Division

**Leon Conference**  
- Hoenn Division
- Sinnoh Division

This structure enables organized competition within divisions while maintaining conference-level standings and playoff qualification.

### Team Structure

Each team consists of:
- **8-10 Pokemon** during the course of a season (minimum 8, maximum 10)
- **11 Pokemon** total roster size (allowing for roster management)
- **Team Name**: Encouraged but not mandatory
- **Coach**: The person managing the team

Teams are assigned to divisions within conferences, creating natural rivalries and organized competition structures.

---

## Season Structure

### Season Timeline

Seasons follow a structured timeline:

1. **Draft Period**: Scheduled draft event where teams select Pokemon
2. **Grace Period**: 1 week after draft for teams to familiarize themselves with rosters
3. **Regular Season**: Weekly battles between teams
4. **Playoffs**: Post-season tournament for top teams
5. **Off-Season**: Period between seasons for rule review and planning

### Season 5 Specifics

## Core Concepts

### Draft Budget System

Teams operate under a point-based budget system:
- **Draft Budget**: 120 points per team (Season 5)
- **Tera Budget**: 15 points per team (Season 5)
- **Point Values**: Pokemon assigned point values from 20 (highest) to 1 (lowest)

This budget system ensures competitive balance and strategic decision-making during drafts.

### Battle Format

- **Format**: 6v6 Singles battles
- **Level Cap**: Level 50 for all Pokemon
- **Scoring**: Wins/losses tracked with point differential
- **Standings**: Based on record and point differential

### Tera System

Season 5 introduces Tera Captains:
- Teams designate 3 Pokemon as Tera Captains
- Each Tera Captain has 3 designated Tera types
- Tera types must be announced by end of day Friday before Week 1
- Tera Captains can be dropped and replaced during the season

## Data Management

### Google Sheets Structure

The league uses Google Sheets for comprehensive data management:

1. **Master Data Sheet**: Core league configuration and data
2. **Draft Board**: Available Pokemon with point values
3. **Teams Pages**: Individual team rosters and information  
4. **Data Sheet**: Calculations, formulas, and derived data
5. **Pokedex**: Pokemon reference information

### Programmatic System

The application provides:
- Battle tracking and results
- Automated standings calculations
- Database storage for league data
- API access for data retrieval
- MCP server for AI integration

## Key Principles

### Competitive Balance

The league is designed to maintain competitive balance through:
- Point-based draft system limiting team power
- Budget constraints preventing overpowered teams
- Structured divisions ensuring varied competition
- Rule enforcement maintaining fair play

### Community Focus

The league emphasizes:
- Respectful competition and sportsmanship
- Long-term community building
- Fun and engaging gameplay
- Collaborative rule development through voting

### Flexibility and Evolution

The league adapts through:
- End-of-season rule reviews
- Mid-season rule amendments (with mod majority)
- Voting systems for rule changes
- Continuous improvement based on community feedback
